Upcoming Events in Indiana

Indiana Dunes Scavenger Hunt

  • Porter, IN
  • January 2 - December 31, 2018

Ready for an adventure? Then do the Indiana Dunes Scavenger Hunt. Pick up the form and map at the Indiana Dunes Visitor Center. You'll be guided to nine great sties in the Indiana Dunes National Lakeshore, where you'll hunt for answers to a series of questions. Answer eight questions and return to the Visitor Center or Paul H. Douglas Center for a free poster featuring the park site logos. Scavenger Hunt is self-guided, and can be done any day that the Visitor Center and the Indiana Dune State Park are open. There is a fee to enter the Indiana Dunes State Park.

Dinosaur Discoveries: Ancient Fossils, New Ideas

  • Muncie, IN
  • February 3 - July 29, 2018

Dinosaur Discoveries: Ancient Fossils, New Ideas is an engaging exhibition that reveals what living, breathing dinosaurs were really like. Using a combination of major fossil finds, captivating computer simulations and provocative models the exhibit introduces a dynamic vision of dinosaurs and the scientists who study them.

"The Reel West" Exhibit

  • Indianapolis, IN
  • March 3 - February 3, 2019
  • 10am - 5pm

The Reel West exhibit explores morality, diversity and American identity as depicted in the Western film genre and how Hollywood movies and television shows shape our understanding of the world around us. Visitors will see film costumes, props, memorabilia, paintings, movie posters and images from the early silent Western films to mid-20th century classics up through today. The Reel West will appeal to casual movie fans, serious film buffs, those who remember iconic Westerns and those new to the genre.

Lois Main Templton: A Reinvented Life

  • Indianapolis, IN
  • March 10 - August 5, 2018

Hoosier artist Lois Main Templeton at 90 years of age remains a force of nature. Her artwork is an extension of her personality: direct, confident, unapologetic and larger-than-life. She combined her love of writing with her passion for painting to create one-of-a-kind works of art that are distinctively hers. In this exhibit, guests will experience 30 of her paintings, see her journals to observe her artistic process and learn about her life. Inspired by her work, visitors will then be able to make their own scratch art or poetry using magnetic words.

Indianapolis Colts: The Exhibit

  • Indianapolis, IN
  • March 10 - January 20, 2020
  • 10am - 5pm

As a new era of professional football in Indiana unfolds, the Indiana Historical Society (IHS) invites guests to connect with the story of the Indianapolis Colts and how the team embodies the values, drive and heart of the city they call home. Indianapolis Colts: The Exhibit uses digital activities, artifacts, player interviews, photographs, videos and more to celebrate the team while also exploring football's role in American culture. Visitors can make a call on a real NFL play and find out if their decision matches the actual call. Another activity lets guests scramble onto the field as mascot Blue to juke and dodge oncoming tackles with their whole body. Indianapolis Colts: The Exhibit debuts March 10 and runs through Jan. 20, 2020, at the Eugene and Marilyn Glick Indiana History Center, located at 450 W. Ohio St. in downtown Indianapolis. The exhibit is included with admission to the Indiana Experience, $9 for adults, $8 for seniors and $5 for kids (ages 5-17). IHS members and children under 5 receive free admission. Indianapolis Colts: The Exhibit is presented by the Indianapolis Colts, with support from Plow Digital. For more information about the exhibit, or other IHS offerings, visit www.indianahistory.org.
